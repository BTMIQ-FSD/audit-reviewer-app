IAS 1 — PRESENTATION OF FINANCIAL STATEMENTS
- Financial statements must present fairly the entity's position, performance and cash flows (para 15).
- Each material class of similar items is presented separately (para 29).
- The notes must be cross-referenced with the face of the statements, and the figures must agree between the face of the statements and the supporting notes (paras 112-116).
- Comparative information is required for the preceding period (para 38).

COMPANIES ACT 2017 (Pakistan) — s.225
Financial statements must give a true and fair view of the state of the company's affairs. Books of account must be properly kept.

ISA 230 — AUDIT DOCUMENTATION
- Documentation must be sufficient for an EXPERIENCED AUDITOR with no previous connection to the audit to understand: the nature, timing and extent of procedures performed; the results and evidence obtained; and significant matters, conclusions, and judgements (para 8).
- Who performed and who reviewed the work, and the dates, must be recorded (para 9).
- Documentation is assembled into the final file on a timely basis (para 14).

ISA 500 — AUDIT EVIDENCE
- The auditor must obtain SUFFICIENT (quantity) and APPROPRIATE (quality: relevance and reliability) audit evidence (para 6).
- Evidence descriptions such as "reviewed and appears reasonable" without stating what was examined and why it supports the conclusion do not demonstrate sufficient appropriate evidence.
- When using samples, the basis of selection and the items tested should be documented (see also ISA 530 on sampling: sample design, size, and selection must be documented, and deviations evaluated).

AUDIT IMPLICATIONS FOR WORKING PAPERS
- Lead schedules must agree exactly with the face of the financial statements; sub-leads must agree with the notes.
- Conclusions must be supported by documented work — a conclusion of "no exceptions" alongside an exception described in the same paper is a contradiction.
- Broken spreadsheet values (#REF!, #VALUE!, #DIV/0!) mean the schedule does not compute and must be fixed before the paper can support any conclusion.
- Sign-offs (preparer, reviewer) with dates are required; reviewer dates should not precede preparer dates.
- Working papers containing another client's name or file reference indicate copy-paste contamination from a reused template and must be corrected.
- Unresolved review queries (e.g. "where is sub lead??") must be cleared before the file is closed.
