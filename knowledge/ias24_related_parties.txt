SOURCE: IAS 24 — Related Party Disclosures
STATUS: Firm summary of key requirements (to be replaced with the firm's licensed official text)

PURPOSE
To ensure financial statements disclose the possibility that the entity's position and results may have been affected by related parties and transactions with them.

WHO IS A RELATED PARTY (para 9)
Includes: entities under common control or ownership; key management personnel and their close family; entities controlled or jointly controlled by such persons.

DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS (paras 13-24)
- Relationships between parents and subsidiaries are disclosed regardless of transactions (para 13).
- Where related party transactions occurred: the NATURE of the relationship, the AMOUNT of the transactions, outstanding BALANCES (including terms, security, guarantees), and provisions for doubtful debts (para 18).
- Disclosures that related party transactions were made on terms equivalent to arm's length are made ONLY if such terms can be substantiated (para 23).

AUDIT IMPLICATIONS FOR A REVENUE WORKING PAPER
- Sales to related parties included in revenue must be identified and disclosed with amounts and relationship nature.
- A claim of arm's-length pricing must be substantiated with evidence, not asserted.
- The working paper should document the pricing basis and the audit procedures performed on related-party sales.

RELATED AUDITING STANDARD: ISA 550 (Related Parties)
Requires the auditor to identify related party relationships and transactions, understand their business rationale, and obtain sufficient appropriate evidence about their accounting and disclosure (ISA 550 paras 13-24).
