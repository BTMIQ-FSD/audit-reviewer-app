CORE PRINCIPLE
Revenue is recognised to depict the transfer of promised goods or services to customers, in an amount reflecting the consideration the entity expects to be entitled to.

THE FIVE-STEP MODEL (paras 9-46)
1. Identify the contract with the customer (para 9): enforceable rights and obligations, commercial substance, collection probable.
2. Identify the performance obligations (paras 22-30): each distinct promise to transfer a good or service.
3. Determine the transaction price (paras 47-72): the consideration the entity expects; exclude amounts collected on behalf of third parties (e.g. sales tax). Variable consideration (discounts, rebates) is estimated and constrained.
4. Allocate the transaction price to the performance obligations (paras 73-90).
5. Recognise revenue when (or as) a performance obligation is satisfied (paras 31-46).

POINT IN TIME vs OVER TIME (paras 35-38)
Revenue is recognised over time only if one of the para 35 criteria is met; otherwise at a point in time when CONTROL transfers (para 38). Indicators of control transfer include: present right to payment, legal title, physical possession, risks and rewards, customer acceptance.

EXPORT SALES (application)
For export sales recognised at a point in time, control typically transfers per shipping terms (e.g. bill of lading date for FOB). Cut-off testing near year end should be supported by shipping documents evidencing the transfer of control.

PRESENTATION / DISCLOSURE (paras 105-129)
Contract assets and contract liabilities are recognised and disclosed; revenue disaggregation is disclosed; amounts included in contract liabilities at the start of the period that were recognised as revenue in the period are disclosed.

AUDIT IMPLICATIONS FOR A REVENUE WORKING PAPER
- The stated recognition policy must match IFRS 15's control-transfer model.
- Where point-in-time recognition is asserted for exports, the working paper should reference the shipping-document evidence tested (cut-off).
- Amounts such as sales tax must be excluded from revenue (transaction price definition).
- Rebates/discounts affect the transaction price and their treatment should be documented.
